**Possible Causes:**

* **Life Events:**  The patient's mention of "struggling from despair and sadness" suggests recent life events that may have triggered these feelings. These could include job loss, relationship difficulties, financial strain, loss of a loved one, or a significant change in life circumstances.
* **Biological Predisposition:**  There is a strong genetic component to depression. Family history of mental illness, particularly depression, can increase the risk of developing the disorder. 
* **Chemical Imbalances:** Neurotransmitters like serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine play a crucial role in mood regulation. Imbalances in these chemicals can contribute to feelings of sadness, despair, and hopelessness.
* **Chronic Stress:**  Prolonged periods of stress can significantly impact mental health, making individuals more susceptible to depression.

**Possible Treatments:**

**1. Psychotherapy:**

* **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT):**  CBT helps individuals identify and challenge negative thoughts and behaviors that contribute to depression. It teaches coping mechanisms and skills to manage symptoms and improve overall well-being.
* **Interpersonal Therapy (IPT):** IPT focuses on improving relationships and social support systems, which are crucial for managing depression. It helps individuals address interpersonal conflicts, communication difficulties, and grief related to loss.
* **Psychodynamic Therapy:** Psychodynamic therapy explores unconscious patterns and past experiences that may contribute to depression. It aims to gain insights into underlying conflicts and develop healthier coping mechanisms.

**2. Medication:**

* **Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs):** SSRIs, such as fluoxetine (Prozac), sertraline (Zoloft), and escitalopram (Lexapro), are the most common antidepressants. They increase serotonin levels in the brain, which helps improve mood.
* **Dosage:** Typical starting doses range from 20mg to 40mg daily, adjusted based on individual response and tolerance.
* **Side Effects:** SSRIs can cause nausea, headaches, insomnia, sexual dysfunction, and weight gain. These side effects usually subside within a few weeks.

**3. Lifestyle Modifications:**

* **Regular Exercise:**  Engaging in regular physical activity can have a positive impact on mood and reduce symptoms of depression. 
* **Healthy Diet:** Consuming a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can improve energy levels and overall well-being.
* **Adequate Sleep:**  Getting enough sleep is crucial for mental health. Aim for 7-8 hours of quality sleep each night.
* **Social Support:**  Connect with friends and family, engage in activities that bring you joy, and seek support from a therapist or support group.
