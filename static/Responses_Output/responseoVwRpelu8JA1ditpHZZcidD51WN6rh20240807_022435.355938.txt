**Treatment Plan:**

**Pharmacological Treatment:**

1. **Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs):** These medications are the first-line treatment for MDD. They work by increasing the levels of serotonin in the brain, which can improve mood and reduce depressive symptoms.

   - **Example:** Sertraline (Zoloft)
      - **Dosage:** Typically starts at 50 mg daily, and can be increased up to 200 mg daily.
      - **Side Effects:** Nausea, headache, drowsiness, dizziness, sexual dysfunction.
      - **Pattern:** Most SSRIs take 2-4 weeks to show noticeable improvement, and full effects can take several months.

2. **Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs):**  These medications work by increasing the levels of both serotonin and norepinephrine in the brain.

   - **Example:** Venlafaxine (Effexor)
      - **Dosage:** Starts at 37.5 mg daily and can be increased up to 375 mg daily.
      - **Side Effects:**  Nausea, headache, dizziness, sweating, increased blood pressure.
      - **Pattern:**  Initial improvement in mood usually seen within 2-4 weeks, but full effects may take several months.

3. **Other Options:** 
   - **Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs):** These older antidepressants are generally less prescribed due to potential side effects, but can be effective in some cases.
   - **Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOIs):** These antidepressants are used less frequently due to food restrictions and potential drug interactions.

**Therapy:** 

1. **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT):**  CBT is a structured type of psychotherapy that helps identify and change negative thoughts and behaviors that contribute to depression. 

2. **Interpersonal Therapy (IPT):** IPT focuses on improving interpersonal relationships and communication skills, which can help manage stress and alleviate depressive symptoms. 

3. **Other Therapy Options:** 
   - **Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT):** Helpful for managing intense emotions and improving self-regulation skills.
   - **Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT):** Focuses on developing mindfulness practices to reduce stress and improve emotional well-being.
